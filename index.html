Introduction

   Argentina is one of the few places thoughout the world that anyone would be proud to call home. Argentina's unqiue foods, grcious wilderness, and stable goverment provides a great place to reside. Overall, Argentina is a diverse and beautiful place to live due to its physical, cultural, and political geographies.

Physcial Features

   Argentina includes majestic mountain tops and beautiful flat plains, making it a marvelous place to reside. To begin, Argentina mainly consists of lowland areas besides the mountains in the west. Topography separates the country into four different regions, those being the Andean, the Patagonia, the subtropical plains, and the pampas region. The Andean region accounts for nearly 30% of the country, and it consists of a high plateau. Additionally, The Patagonia region makes up the area from the Río Negro territory of Argentina to the southern part of the country (“Argentina”). All in all, these features make Argentina a place worth moving to.

Climate

   To continue, Argentina’s climate is one of a temperate nature, which makes it the perfect place to live. In Argentina, climate is pretty level headed overall, but depending on where you are, things could vary. The temperature in Buenos Aires averages around 61 degrees Fahrenheit, or 16 degrees Celsius. Furthermore, January is one of Argentina’s warmest months, but June and July are some of its coldest months. To add on, Buenos Aires gets around 37 inches (94 centimeters) of rain each year (“Argentina”). Overall, Argentina’s climate is one to be desired, making it a prime place to reside in.

Water Features

   Argentina’s unique water features make it a wondrous place to settle down and live in. The main rivers in Argentina all flow central intro the central Río de la Plata river, which lies between Uruguay and Argentina. To elaborate, The mouth of the Río de la Plata river can reach a maximum of nearly 138 miles. In addition, A region of snow-fed lakes lie near the bottom of the Andes Mountains. Furthermore, A number of small salt lakes are located in the provinces Buenos Aires, La Pampa, and Córdoba (“Argentina”). In conclusion, the water features that await you in Argentina make it an astonishing place to live in.

City Life

   In Argentina, most middle-classed people normally live in urban areas, such as cities, creating a great community to live in. First of all, living in the city offers a standard way of living in Argentina. Argentines mainly live in urban areas due to the conveniency they possess.,which makes city life the place where practically everything happens. Additionally, 1940 was the year that industry had drawn people to live in more urban environments. Finally, people also tend to go to urban cities due to their influence on other Spanish cities (Jokisch). To conclude, living in an Argentinean city provides a stable and constructive place to live.

Food

   Argentina’s delectable foods support why you should live in Argentina. In the country of Argentina, beef is one of the many food specialties it has. Furthermore, Asado is a very common way to cook beef/meat. There are several things the asado may include, such as pork, chicken, sausages, prime ribs of beef, etcetera. In addition, there is also a dish called Empanadas, which are pastries filled with things such as cheese, vegetables, and ground meat. This meal is mainly served as an appetizer in Argentina (Jokisch). Thus, Argentina’s delectably delicious delicacies create an undeniable reason to move to Argentina.

Recreation

   In Argentina, recreation mainly consists of sports and other outdoor activities. First of all, Argentina’s most popular sport is fútbol, or soccer. In addition, Argentines play other sports including basketball, rugby, automobile racing, and horse racing. To continue, the Atlantic south coast of Buenos Aires and the hilly country around Córdoba province are popular vacation spots. Finally, the southern Andes attract hikers, hunters, and skiers (Jokisch). On the whole, Argentina’s wide number of sports provides a great place to live.

Education

   Argentina’s education system supplies a sufficient education for anyone, adult or child, to succeed. For starters, most of all Argentines that are older than 15 have the ability to read and write. This is due to the fact that the government provides free public elementary and high school education, and that any child between the ages of 5 and 14 must go to school (Jokisch).Additionally, A portion of the Argentinian school system is based off of the United States, but a large percentage is based off of France’s. To add on, formal education is required from the ages of 6 to 14. Finally, The teachers are trained in a regular school setting, and receive an education from a university (Hanson). Overall, Argentina’s education superb system supports why you should move to Argentina.

Government

   Argentina’s government provides a stable foundation for any nation to prosper, and any citizen to live. To begin with, Argentina’s government is modeled after the United State’s system. Their government is a democratic republic, and includes three branches--executive, legislative, and judicial. Furthermore, Argentina has 23 provinces which all have an elected governor, legislature, its own constitution. In addition, Argentina’s two largest political parties are the Justicialist Party(PJ), and the Radical Civic Union (UCR). The PJ is more labor oriented, while the UCR represents the middle-class voters (Jokisch). All in all, the Argentine government system provides a stable country to reside in, which makes it a great place to live.

Military

   Argentina’s armed forces provide a secure location for anyone who wishes to live in Argentina. As of 2011, there were around 73,100 members in Argentina’s armed forces. To break things down, there were around 38,500 people in the army, 20,000 people in the navy, and 14,600 in the air force. Once the 1900s were reached, military service was optional instead of being forced (“Argentina"). In addition, once the foundation for a healthy and stable government was formed, Argentina’s military was gaining power. Thanks to the aid of missions from Germany and Britain, the military was able to receive training from the foreign countries, further growing its power (Hanson). In conclusion, Argentina’s armed forces provide citizens with a safe area to live, and makes Argentina a great place to move to.

Conclusion

   Argentina is a diverse and beautiful place to live due to its physical, cultural, and political geographies. Argentina’s beautiful and unique mountains and plains make it an excellent place to reside. Additionally, Argentina’s food, recreation, and urban areas make it a wondrous place to move to. Furthermore, the political, educational, and military systems make Argentina a safe, stable, and secure place to settle down. All in all, Argentina would be a great place to immigrate to and live a happy life.
